*In the wild* means leaving the petri dish. It means systems that must keep
working when the sensor is muddy, the population drifts, the substrate is
alive, and the experimenter has gone home.

---

Artificial life has always been a discipline of *as it could be*. But the
things we now build — open-ended evolutionary systems, embodied agents,
biohybrids, environmental computers, generative ecologies — increasingly
leave the simulator and meet the world. They sit in rivers, on rooftops,
inside soil, on satellites, on phones, in the dirt of other species.

This workshop gathers researchers, artists, and field practitioners who are
taking ALife out of the controlled regime and into the open one. We are
interested in the *messy* parts: failure modes that only appear after week
six, evaluation when there is no ground truth, ethics when the agent is
alive, and the strange new science that emerges when an ALife system has
neighbours.

We invite contributions that are speculative, unfinished, weird, or
empirically grounded. We especially welcome work that did not survive the
lab.
